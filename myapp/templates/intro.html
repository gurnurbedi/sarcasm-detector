LSTN Model

The LSTN model is a variant of the Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM) network. It is designed for sequence prediction problems and is widely used in natural language processing (NLP) tasks.

This model captures the temporal dependencies in data and is known for its ability to handle long-range dependencies effectively.

XLNET Model

XLNET is a state-of-the-art language model developed by researchers at Google AI Brain and Carnegie Mellon University. It's designed to outperform previous models like BERT by leveraging a novel pre-training method that combines the best aspects of autoregressive and autoencoding models.

XLNET can handle a wide range of natural language processing tasks with high accuracy, including text classification, sentiment analysis, and question answering.

RoBERTa Model

RoBERTa stands for "Robustly optimized BERT approach." It's a natural language processing (NLP) model developed by Facebook AI. Think of it as a smarter version of the BERT model that has been fine-tuned to perform even better.

RoBERTa reads and learns from a massive amount of text (like books, articles, and websites) to understand how words and sentences work.

About WittyBot

WittyBot is an intelligent bot designed to detect and respond to sarcasm in text. It uses advanced natural language processing techniques to understand the nuances of human language, making interactions more natural and engaging.

WittyBot can be used in various applications, including customer support, virtual tutoring, and personal assistance, providing more accurate and empathetic responses.
